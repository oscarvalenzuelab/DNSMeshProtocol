What dig actually does

    participant R as 1.1.1.1

    Bob->>R: TXT? id-{hash}.mesh.gnu.cl
    alt cached
      R-->>Bob: cached value (within TTL)

      R->>Roots: who serves cl?
      Roots-->>R: ns.nic.cl
      R->>Roots: who serves gnu.cl?
      Roots-->>R: ns1.digitalocean.com
      R->>DO: TXT? id-...mesh.gnu.cl
      DO-->>R: NS dnsmesh.io for mesh.gnu.cl
      R->>Node: TXT? id-...mesh.gnu.cl
      Node-->>R: "v=dmp1;t=identity;d=..."
      R-->>Bob: same record
    end
    Bob->>Bob: parse + verify Ed25519 signature

Three things make this fast

  1. Resolver caching. 1.1.1.1 sees billions of queries — >95% cache hit rates are typical.
  2. Short TTLs (60s default). Churn visible quickly without hammering the authoritative node.
  3. Local signature verification. Bob's CLI doesn't trust the resolver, the path, or the node — only the Ed25519 signature on the record bytes.

Why dnsmesh init defaults to 1.1.1.1

dns_resolvers:
  - 1.1.1.1     # Cloudflare
  - 8.8.8.8     # Google (failover)
  • Skip stale negative cache. Your ISP's resolver might still hold "NXDOMAIN" from before a delegation existed. Public resolvers' caches are fresher.
  • Failover. ResolverPool quietly fails over between the two if one stops responding.
  • Reproducibility. Triage is faster when "what resolver?" has a known answer.

Privacy-conscious deploys: dnsmesh init --no-default-resolvers to fall back to your local resolver.

Querying resolver vs node directly

Through a public resolver

dig @1.1.1.1 \
    id-{hash}.mesh.gnu.cl \
    TXT +short

What everyone else sees. Goes through the full resolution chain.

Direct to the node

dig @dnsmesh.io \
    id-{hash}.mesh.gnu.cl \
    TXT +short

Source of truth. Bypasses every cache. If this returns nothing but the resolver did, you're seeing stale cache upstream.

Visible outage = TTL (60s) + maybe a few minutes of negative-cache lag. From a normal client's view, the address just stops resolving.

Sends fail immediately. No retry queue. Pending sends do not survive a node restart unless tooling persists them.

Writes fan to ⌈N/2⌉ of the named nodes (single-node failure survivable). Reads union across all N. When node-b returns, anti-entropy walks the cursor since its last sync and ingests everything it missed.

Things people actually ask

  • "dig @dnsmesh.io works but my CLI doesn't" — caching upstream. Try @1.1.1.1 or wait for the negative-cache TTL.
  • "How long until everyone sees my publish?" — As long as the longest negative-cache TTL on the path. ~3 min for active resolvers, up to 15 min for slow caches.
  • "Should I run my own resolver to avoid leaking my contact list to Cloudflare?" — Maybe. Public resolvers see which DMP names you query, not message contents (E2EE). If that's a concern: dns_resolvers: ['127.0.0.1'].
